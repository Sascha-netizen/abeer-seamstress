My story...

  • I was born and raised in Cairo, but my family is orginally from Menofia in the Nile Delta.
  • After completing my Egyptian high school diploma, or 'Thanaweya Amma', I attended Ain Shams University, where I studied commerce.
  • For a few years, I worked in marketing and accounting but found no fulfillment in spreadsheets and figures.
  • Feeling bold, I joined The International School of Choueifat, New Cairo as an exam officer.
  • At around that time, I also started to explore arts and crafts, such as photography, painting and sewing.
  • Sewing has become my passion, and I have crafted all sorts of sartorial items for both myself and a wide range of clients.
  • I eventually married one of the expat teachers at my school, Dr Sascha R Klement.
  • We recently moved to Germany, where I work in the Frankfurt branch of Choueifat.
  • But since sewing is my passion, I decided to pursue it semi-professionally as a self-employed seamstress.
  • My husband has now left the teaching profession and works as web developer.
  • I craft bespoke fashion for Muslim girls and women, who proudly and confidently practice their faith.
  • Whenever time allows, my husband and I attend a local mosque, which caters to Muslims from diverse backgrounds.
  • Don't hesitate to get in touch if you're looking for handmade modest fashion!
